MegascopeV2

History :

MegascopeV2 was developed by font designer David Johnathon Ross (DJR) in 2023. The font exists as an addition to DJR's series of "Megafonts" that originally drew inspiration from science-fiction elements and Deco-geometric sans-serif fonts of the 1970s. MegascopeV2, a more experimental version of "Megascope,"" is characterized by small circles in the B/P/R letters, diagonals that stick to a 60° angle, and a tight but not touching style of spacing. This visual font tests the breaking point of Megascope and pushes the weight settings thin, light, regular, medium, and bold as far as possible. The unique "scope" variable axis enlarges the circular forms within the letters, ultimately creating round, exaggerated proportions.

What is an axis in variable fonts?

Variable font files contain multiple stylistic variations such as Weight, Width, Style, Size, etc. These variable elements are controlled by axes specific to each font that can be manipulated and combined by the user.
